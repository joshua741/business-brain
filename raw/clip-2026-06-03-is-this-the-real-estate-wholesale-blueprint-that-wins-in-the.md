# Is This the Real Estate Wholesale Blueprint That Wins in the Next Market?
**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1oZMa96zfHY   **Duration:** 00:16:21

## Summary
The video presents a "2026 Blueprint" for real estate investors, regardless of their experience level or investment strategy (wholesaler, fix & flipper, buy & hold). The speaker, Matt Beard, emphasizes that the upcoming real estate market in 2026 is projected to be more affordable with increased inventory and sales, making it a prime opportunity for investors. He introduces his "No Fluff Model," a three-phase system designed to help investors find and close off-market deals efficiently and cost-effectively, primarily by building relationships with real estate agents.

The core thrust of the model is to position investors as solutions for realtors, rather than competitors, by offering to handle "funky properties" that agents might struggle to sell on the traditional market. This approach aims to significantly reduce lead generation costs compared to traditional methods like direct mail or cold calling. The video breaks down the process into detailed steps for lead generation, lead conversion, and deal disposition, providing specific metrics and strategies for each phase, including leveraging AI for initial outreach and focusing on rapid deal execution.

## Key Takeaways
-   The 2026 real estate market is predicted to be more affordable with increased inventory (8.9% increase in existing homes, 3.1% in new single-family homes) and more sales, presenting a significant opportunity for investors.
-   The best off-market real estate opportunities come from realtors, not direct mail, cold calling, or texting homeowners.
-   By working with realtors, investors can find deals for significantly less cost (e.g., $100-$150 per deal) compared to traditional wholesale methods ($2,000-$10,000 per deal).
-   Investors should position themselves as a "solution" for realtors, offering value beyond just asking for deals, especially for properties that are difficult to sell.
-   The "No Fluff Model" is a three-phase process: Lead Generation, Lead Conversion, and Deal Disposition.
-   Consistency in outreach and follow-up is crucial for long-term success and leveraging the compounding effect in real estate.
-   New investors should consider joint venture (JV) partnerships to overcome knowledge or skill gaps, allowing them to focus on lead generation and conversion.

## Frameworks / SOPs
**No Fluff Model**
This is a three-phase system for real estate investors to find and close off-market deals, primarily by working with real estate agents.

**Phase 1: Lead Generation**
This phase focuses on efficiently identifying potential off-market properties through real estate agents.
1.  **Texting & Emailing Agents at Scale**: Utilize a system to send out mass text messages and emails to real estate agents in target markets.
2.  **AI Bot for Filtering Responses**: An AI bot is used to filter responses from agents.
    *   **No Answer**: Agents who don't respond are put into follow-up campaigns.
    *   **Cash Agent Dead**: Agents who explicitly state they are not interested or have negative responses are tagged as "Cash Agent Dead" and moved to a dead bucket.
    *   **Interested / Have a conversation with...**: Agents who show interest or provide an address are categorized into three tiers:
        *   **Tier 1**: Agents who provide an address (on-market or off-market). These are prioritized.
        *   **Tier 2**: Agents who are interested but not actively working a deal, or whose provided deal didn't work out. They are moved into an automated follow-up sequence.
        *   **Tier 3**: Agents who do not have a deal and no address but are open to future contact. They are also placed in an automated follow-up sequence.
3.  **Focus on Off-Market Properties**: Prioritize properties that are off-market, as they typically offer better deals.
4.  **Metrics**:
    *   500 new texts/day to agents.
    *   Yields 3-8 leads (properties) per day.
    *   22-40% of these leads are off-market.
    *   12-20 leads typically convert into one deal.

**Phase 2: Lead Conversion (The 3 Cs)**
This phase focuses on converting leads into contracts. The three steps are: Call, Comp, Close.
1.  **Call (Lead Management Call)**:
    *   Call the agent as soon as possible (ASAP, within 6 hours) after receiving an address.
    *   The primary goal is to build rapport and "lay of the land," not to present an offer immediately.
    *   Ask about existing offers, the agent's opinion on the property's value after repairs, and the estimated repair work needed (kitchen, bathrooms, etc.).
    *   Respect the agent's opinion and avoid trying to educate or bash their knowledge.
    *   Set the expectation that you will do your homework and might be "off" on their asking price, but are flexible.
2.  **Comp (Underwrite)**:
    *   After the initial call, underwrite the property to determine the maximum viable offer.
    *   This should be done quickly, ideally within 12 hours.
    *   The "gold standard" for a property is: 1. Off Market, 2. Ugly House, 3. Asking Price Under Zillow's Zestimate.
3.  **Close (Negotiate)**:
    *   Present an offer only when the agent is ready to make a decision.
    *   This offer doesn't necessarily need to be in writing initially; a ballpark number can be given over the phone.
    *   Build trust with the agent by being reliable and doing what you say you will do, so they will advocate for your offer to their client.

**Phase 3: Deal Disposition**
This phase focuses on selling the contracted property.
1.  **JV Partner (for new investors)**: If you are new and lack a buyer's list or experience in underwriting/selling, partner with someone who does. This allows you to focus on Phases 1 and 2.
2.  **Time To Blast (TTB) Metric**: Track the time from getting a property under contract to sending it out to buyers.
    *   A TTB of less than 12 hours significantly increases the likelihood of selling a deal (over 400% increase based on internal tracking).
3.  **Pipeline Management**: Maintain a strong and consistent pipeline of deals, as not all contracts will close (historically, 60-65% close if done right). This ensures continuous income even if some deals fall apart.

## Action Items
-   Study and implement the "No Fluff Model" for real estate investing.
-   Join the "No Fluff Real Estate" community for additional resources, training, and challenges.
-   Focus on consistent texting and emailing to real estate agents to generate leads.
-   Utilize an AI bot or similar system to filter agent responses and categorize leads into Tiers.
-   Prioritize off-market properties and those meeting the "gold standard" criteria (off-market, ugly house, asking price under Zestimate).
-   For Tier 1 leads (agents providing addresses), make a "Lead Management Call" as soon as possible (within 6 hours) to build rapport and gather information.
-   Quickly underwrite (comp) properties, ideally within 12 hours, to determine a viable offer.
-   Present offers strategically, only when the agent is ready to make a decision, and focus on building trust.
-   If new to deal disposition, consider partnering with a JV partner who has an established buyer's list and experience.
-   Track the "Time To Blast" (TTB) metric and aim to get properties under contract and sent out to buyers in less than 12 hours.
-   Maintain a strong and consistent deal pipeline to mitigate the impact of deals falling apart.
-   Download and utilize the 30-day guide and scripts provided in the community description to improve communication with realtors.
