# The New Way of Making Content In The Age of AI
**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XsWSvz-aewA   **Duration:** 00:13:53

## Summary
This video explores how to build a resilient personal brand in 2026, particularly in the context of advancing Artificial Intelligence (AI). The speaker, Alex Hormozi, argues that AI will not equally disrupt all content creators; instead, it will disproportionately impact those in "low-risk" content categories while struggling to replicate the credibility required for "high-risk" content. He introduces a continuum ranging from low-risk (entertainers) to high-risk (B2B creators), explaining that the higher the stakes for the consumer, the more crucial real-world proof and demonstration become.

Hormozi emphasizes that to thrive amidst AI, creators must focus on generating and showcasing authentic proof of their expertise and results. This involves integrating demonstration directly into existing business operations, creating a "self-licking ice cream cone" where customer interactions and successes are documented and then used as content. He advises against merely creating content from scratch and instead advocates for "doing epic shit and documenting it," ensuring that personal brands are built on verifiable experience that AI cannot easily fake or replicate.

## Key Takeaways
- AI will disproportionately disrupt content creators in low-risk categories (e.g., entertainment, B2C education) more than those in high-risk categories (e.g., B2Prosumer, B2B creators).
- The level of risk a consumer takes when engaging with content directly correlates with the amount of proof and credibility required from the creator.
- For high-risk content, authentic real-world experience, demonstration, and verifiable results are paramount for building trust and influence.
- To future-proof a personal brand against AI, focus on engineering proof and demonstration into your core business operations.
- Instead of creating content from scratch, "do epic shit and document it" by capturing and sharing real-time customer interactions, results, and expertise.
- If you don't have enough existing customer proof, create opportunities for public demonstration, such as offering free services or audits and documenting the process.

## Frameworks / SOPs
-   **Low Risk to High Risk Continuum for Creators**: This framework categorizes content creators based on the level of risk consumers undertake when engaging with their content.
    -   **Low Risk (Entertainers)**: Content where the primary risk is the time spent consuming it (e.g., memes, comedy clips). The value is self-contained within the consumption. AI is best suited to disrupt this category.
    -   **B2C Educator**: Content aimed at changing consumer behavior with relatively low personal stakes (e.g., hair tutorials, diet advice, relationship tips). While there's a risk of the advice not working, the consequences are generally not catastrophic. AI can also disrupt this category.
    -   **B2Prosumer**: Content involving higher stakes, often related to significant personal investments or financial decisions (e.g., savings, investing money). Credibility from real-world expertise (e.g., an attorney or finance professional) becomes more important. AI will find it harder to disrupt this.
    -   **B2B Creators**: Content for businesses, typically involving high financial stakes and critical business decisions. This category demands substantial proof and demonstration of real-world success (e.g., building and scaling companies, sales teams). AI will find it most difficult to disrupt this category due to the need for authentic, human-led proof and experience.
-   **"Do Epic Shit and Document It"**: This is a content creation strategy where instead of fabricating content or stories, one focuses on achieving significant real-world results or experiences ("epic shit") and then documents these achievements and the processes involved to generate authentic content.
-   **"Self-Licking Ice Cream Cone"**: A concept describing a business model or process where each component naturally feeds into and reinforces the others, creating a continuous, self-sustaining loop of value creation, marketing, and proof generation. For content, this means integrating the generation of proof and its documentation directly into the delivery of products or services.

## Action Items
- Identify where your current content and personal brand fall on the low-risk to high-risk continuum.
- For content in the higher-risk categories, prioritize building and demonstrating real-world proof and credibility.
- Engineer opportunities for demonstration and proof directly into your existing business operations and customer service processes.
- Document your customer interactions, results, and the value you provide in real-time to generate content.
- If you currently lack sufficient customer proof, consider offering free services or audits to potential clients and publicly document the process and results.
- Actively seek to "do epic shit and document it" by focusing on achieving significant results and then capturing those experiences as content.
