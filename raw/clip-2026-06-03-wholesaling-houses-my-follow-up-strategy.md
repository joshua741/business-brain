# Wholesaling Houses: My follow up Strategy
**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aVyVsmz4Gvk   **Duration:** 00:05:35

## Summary
This video provides a comprehensive guide to effective follow-up strategies in real estate wholesaling, emphasizing its critical role in closing deals and maximizing profits. The speaker, with over $60 million in wholesale transactions, shares his proven methods for nurturing leads through consistent communication and strategic pipeline management. He highlights that proper follow-up can add hundreds of thousands of dollars to a wholesaling business.

The core of the strategy involves an aggressive initial follow-up period, followed by a structured tapering if no momentum is gained. Beyond just calling, the video stresses the importance of qualifying leads effectively by asking two critical questions: what type of deal it could be and when it could realistically happen. It also delves into the necessity of handling objections upfront to prevent sellers from disengaging and maintaining an efficient, organized CRM to streamline the follow-up process.

## Key Takeaways
- Consistent and aggressive follow-up is crucial for closing deals and maximizing profits in wholesaling.
- Implement a "triple dial" strategy: call three times in quick succession if there's no answer, followed by a voicemail and text.
- Maintain a structured follow-up schedule: daily for two weeks, then weekly for four weeks, then monthly.
- Qualify leads by determining the potential deal type and realistic timeline before adding them to the daily follow-up pipeline.
- Address and resolve all seller objections fully before ending a call to prevent disengagement.
- Keep clear, concise, and organized notes in your CRM to ensure efficiency and avoid wasting time.
- Speed equals money, and efficiency equals scale in managing your pipeline.
- Use personalized selfie videos with a "big claim" to re-engage unresponsive sellers.

## Frameworks / SOPs
**Follow-Up Strategy:**
1.  **Initial Aggressive Phase (First 2 Weeks):**
    *   **Call every single day.**
    *   **Triple Dial:** If no answer on the first call, call again immediately. If still no answer, call a third time.
    *   **Leave Voicemail & Text:** Every single time there's no answer.
2.  **Intermediate Phase (Next 4 Weeks):**
    *   If no connection or momentum after two weeks of daily calls, shift to calling **once a week** for four weeks.
3.  **Long-Term Phase:**
    *   If still no response after the intermediate phase, move the lead to a **once-a-month follow-up**.

**Lead Qualification Process:**
Before a prospect enters the daily follow-up pipeline, answer these two critical questions:
1.  **What type of deal could this be?** (e.g., wholesale, creative finance, renovation, listing program)
2.  **When could this happen realistically?** (e.g., urgent - next week/month, or long-term - several months/years)

**Objection Handling Rule:**
*   Handle every objection fully before hanging up the phone. If a seller hesitates, dig in to understand the core issue (price, terms, personal, other issues).

**CRM Efficiency Principle:**
*   Keep clear, concise, organized notes in your CRM. Notes should immediately indicate what has happened and what needs to happen on the next call, eliminating the need to dig through past conversations.

## Action Items
- Implement the triple-dial follow-up strategy for new offers or existing offers: call daily for two weeks (triple dial, voicemail, text), then weekly for four weeks, then monthly.
- Before adding a prospect to the daily follow-up pipeline, answer two critical questions: "What type of deal could this be?" and "When could this happen realistically?"
- Practice handling objections immediately and fully during calls, digging into the seller's core hesitation.
- Maintain a highly organized CRM with clear, concise notes that instantly show past interactions and next steps for each lead.
- For unresponsive sellers, send a personalized selfie video with a "big claim" (e.g., "I have a better offer" or "new terms") to re-engage them.
