# Limited Power of Attorney for Bankruptcy Proceedings and Related Real Estate Matters

**Description:** Limited power of attorney for bankruptcy situations — creative finance document.
**Governing Law:** Texas
**Type:** Fillable template

## Parties
- **Principal No. 1** (Granting Party) — homeowner/seller in bankruptcy
- **Principal No. 2** (Co-Granting Party, if applicable)
- **Agent / Attorney-in-Fact** — authorized party (investor)

## Scope of Authority Granted

1. **Lender & Servicer Communications** — Agent can communicate with mortgage servicers, loss mitigation departments; request loan info, submit applications, negotiate modifications, forbearance, short payoff terms

2. **Document Submission & Retrieval** — Submit/retrieve documents for lenders, servicers, trustees, courts; financial statements, hardship letters, income documentation

3. **Real Property Transactions** — Negotiate/prepare contracts, purchase agreements, listing agreements related to identified property; requires prior written approval for any agreement involving purchase/sale price

4. **Coordination with Bankruptcy Attorney/Trustee** — Communicate with retained bankruptcy attorney, trustee, court professionals; receive case documents, pleadings, schedules; participate in strategy sessions (cannot file pleadings or appear as counsel)

5. **Loss Mitigation Coordination** — Loan modification applications, deed-in-lieu, short sale facilitation, forbearance requests, repayment plan discussions

6. **Execution of Ancillary Documents** — Third-party authorizations, income/tax documentation forms, servicer-required forms (CANNOT execute deeds, mortgages, promissory notes without separate authorization)

## Limitations (Expressly Excluded)
- Cannot file bankruptcy petitions, motions, or pleadings
- Cannot execute deeds, mortgages, or instruments transferring ownership
- Cannot settle, compromise, or waive legal claims
- Cannot withdraw funds from Principal's accounts
- Cannot make legal representations to court or trustee
- Cannot sign agreements creating personal financial obligations without prior written approval
- Cannot contradict or circumvent court/trustee authority

## Term & Termination
Effective upon execution; terminates upon:
1. Written revocation by Principal
2. Discharge, dismissal, or final closing of Bankruptcy Proceedings
3. Death or legal incapacitation of Principal
4. Specific expiration date (if entered)

## Application in Creative Finance
- Used when buying properties subject-to or via seller finance where seller is in or approaching bankruptcy
- Allows investor to manage lender communications and loss mitigation without seller needing to be present for every interaction
- Requires notarization
