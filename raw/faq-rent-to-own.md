# FAQ Rent to Own

**Description:** Detailed FAQ document for rent-to-own buyer inquiries — covers program terms, qualification requirements, seller financing comparison, pet policy, and application process for Rent 2 Own Cribs.

---

### **Rent-to-Own Program — Common Questions**

**What is Rent-to-Own?**
Our Rent-to-Own program is designed to make the path to homeownership simple and flexible. It's basically a lease agreement that gives you the option to buy the home later.

When you enter the program, you'll pay a deposit (called an "option fee") that locks in the purchase price and secures your exclusive right to buy the home. That way, no one else can purchase it while you're in the agreement, and you get time to decide if the home is the right long-term fit.

**What Does Equitable Interest Mean?**
When you join our Rent-to-Own program, you don't officially own the home yet, but you do hold what's called *equitable interest.*

That means you have a legal interest in the property, even though the title stays in the seller's name until you buy it. During this time, the seller can't sell the home to anyone else, and you'll take care of the home just like an owner would — including handling any repairs or upkeep while you're living there.

**How is Rent-to-Own Different from Seller Financing?**
Both Rent-to-Own and Seller Financing help you buy a home, but the way they work is a little different.

- **Rent-to-Own** gives you the flexibility to lease the home for a while and decide later if you want to buy it. If you choose to move forward with the purchase, you can either get a traditional mortgage or finance directly with us through Seller Financing — as long as your payment history has stayed above 80% on-time.

  One important thing to know: while your monthly payments are set up to match what your future mortgage would look like, they aren't applied toward the purchase price with the exception of your initial deposit. The sale price is locked in from day one.

- **Seller Financing** is for buyers who are ready to become homeowners right away. You'll make monthly mortgage payments directly to the seller (instead of a bank) and the ownership title will transfer to you at closing. Payments are structured just like a regular mortgage, typically with principal and interest.

**When is Seller Financing a Good Fit?**
Seller Financing is a great option if you've already got your down payment saved and you're ready to take full ownership of the home. If you're not quite there yet, or if you want some time to make sure the home is right for you, our Rent-to-Own program gives you breathing room.

It also gives you the chance to work toward qualifying for Seller Financing through your on-time payment history.

**What Are the Terms of a Rent-to-Own Agreement?**
Rent-to-Own agreements are usually set up for one year at a time, with the option to renew. To start the agreement, we typically require a deposit between **$3,500 and $5,000** — depending on the home's price. That deposit goes toward the final purchase price when you're ready to buy.

**What Do I Need to Qualify?**
To qualify for our Rent-to-Own program, you'll need to meet some basic criteria:

- A **credit score of 520 or higher**
- Monthly income that's at least **2.5 times the rent payment**
- No **foreclosures** in the last 5 years
- No **bankruptcies** in the last 2 years
- No **evictions or judgments** related to rental history in the last 2-5 years

We'll also ask for documents that show how you earn income — things like pay stubs, tax returns, or bank statements depending on your situation.

**How Can I Qualify for Seller Financing While in Rent-to-Own?**
If you're renting-to-own and want to switch over to Seller Financing, keeping a strong payment history is key. We look for at least **80% on-time payments** during your lease term.

If late payments start stacking up, it can affect your ability to qualify or may mean an additional deposit is required before moving forward.

**How Are Rent-to-Own Payments Calculated?**
Your monthly Rent-to-Own payment is designed to be about the same as what your future mortgage payment would be, based on the home's price and interest rate at the start of the agreement.

**Will My Payments Change Over Time?**
Your payment can change, but only when property taxes or insurance costs go up. These are part of your escrow payments, which are rolled into your monthly total.

**Why Do We Offer Rent-to-Own?**
Our goal is to make homeownership possible for more families, especially for people who might not qualify for a mortgage right away or who just want a little more time before fully committing to buying.

**How long can I stay in a Rent-to-Own agreement before I have to buy the home?**
Our Rent-to-Own agreements are set up one year at a time, and they can be renewed annually as long as both sides agree. There's no pressure to buy before you're ready — the option to purchase stays open for the full term of your agreement.

**Can I purchase the home early before my lease term is over?**
Absolutely! If you decide you're ready to buy before your lease is up, you can exercise your option to purchase at any time during the agreement.

**What happens if I decide not to buy the home?**
If you decide the home isn't the right fit, you're not locked into buying it. You'll simply complete your lease term and move on. The deposit (option fee) is non-refundable, as it secured your exclusive purchase rights.

**What if my financial situation changes while I'm renting?**
We understand life happens. If you're facing financial changes, the most important thing is to communicate with us early. Staying on top of payments (especially keeping your on-time percentage above 80%) is the key to keeping your path to ownership open.

**Who pays for repairs during a Rent-to-Own agreement?**
Once you're in the Rent-to-Own program, you're responsible for the upkeep of the property — just like a homeowner would be.

**Can I make changes or improvements to the home while I'm renting?**
Yes, with some basic guidelines. Cosmetic updates like painting, landscaping, or adding personal touches are usually fine, but anything structural or major should always be approved by us first.

**Is the deposit applied toward the purchase price?**
Yes! The deposit you pay at the beginning (your "option fee") will be credited toward the final purchase price when you're ready to buy.

**Can the purchase price change during my Rent-to-Own agreement?**
No — the purchase price is locked in when you sign the agreement, even if the market value of the home goes up while you're still leasing.

**Do I need to get approved for a mortgage before starting Rent-to-Own?**
Nope! That's one of the biggest advantages of Rent-to-Own. You don't need to qualify for a mortgage upfront.

**What happens if I fall behind on payments?**
Staying current on your payments is super important in a Rent-to-Own agreement — especially if you plan to purchase the home later.

**Will my monthly payments be applied toward the purchase price?**
No — in our Rent-to-Own program, the monthly lease payments are not applied toward the purchase price. The option fee is the part that gets credited when you decide to buy.

**Can I refinance with a traditional lender before the Rent-to-Own term ends?**
Yes, you can refinance at any time during the lease term once you qualify for financing.

**What happens if the market value of the home drops before I buy?**
Because the purchase price is locked in when you sign, it won't change if the market fluctuates. If prices go down, you still have the choice to walk away at the end of your term.

**What happens if the market value of the home goes up before I buy?**
If the home's value increases, you benefit from the built-in equity once you purchase at the agreed-upon price.

**Does Rent-to-Own affect my credit score?**
Rent-to-Own agreements themselves are not reported to credit bureaus, but on-time payments help you prepare for future financing.

**Are property taxes and insurance included in my monthly payment?**
Yes — your Rent-to-Own payment is designed to mirror what your future mortgage payment would look like, including property taxes and insurance.

**Do you report my on-time payments to credit bureaus?**
Currently, we don't report Rent-to-Own payments to credit bureaus, but your lender can see your payment history when you apply for financing.

**Can I split the down payment and pay it along with my rent over time?**
No, the full down payment must be paid before move-in.

**What type of homes do you guys offer?**
We primarily offer single family homes. Visit our available properties section to see what we have available today.

## Pet Policy

SERVICE ANIMALS: With proper documentation, service and assistance animals are exempt from any rent, fees, deposits, breed restrictions, and insurance requirements.

ANIMALS: No more than three (3) pets are allowed per home (subject to local laws and HOA Rules and Regulations). Pet fees and/or deposits will be charged for dogs and cats. Dog Bite Liability Insurance is required.

Dogs: Restricted breeds include American Pit Bull Terriers, Rottweilers, Staffordshire Terriers, and any dog that has a percentage or mix of any of those breeds.

Cats: All breeds of domestic cats are permitted.

Small animals: Gerbils, hamsters, and guinea pigs are permitted. Ferrets, rabbits, and chinchillas are not permitted.

Birds: Domestic birds, such as cockatiels and parakeets, are permitted.

Fish tanks: Limited to 20 gallons.

Up to three cats and dogs are welcome with a $150 initial fee per pet and $25 additional monthly pet rent for each pet.
