## Summary
This video features Jerry Norton interviewing "Hu" (likely Hugh), a real estate investor who has built a million-dollar-a-year business solely through working with real estate agents. ## Key Takeaways
-   The agent model can be a highly profitable primary business strategy, capable of generating over $1 million per year, not just a temporary solution.
-   Building strong relationships with real estate agents can lead to consistent off-market deal flow without a marketing budget.
-   When contacting agents, do not immediately identify as an "investor" to avoid negative preconceptions; instead, state you are "looking to buy a home."
-   A systematic texting sequence (e.g., 5 texts, 60-day break, 5 more texts) is effective for initial outreach and follow-up with agents.
-   The goal of initial text outreach is to get agents to call you, rather than engaging in lengthy text conversations or cold calling them.
-   It takes approximately 6-9 months to ramp up the agent pipeline to achieve consistent deal flow.
-   A small, efficient team (e.g., 2.5 acquisition managers and 1.5 disposers) can manage a high volume of deals.
-   Leveraging a construction team (or cash buyers) as "partners" in the conversation with agents helps frame the deal as a collaborative effort, making assignments smoother.

## Frameworks / SOPs
-   **Agent Outreach Texting Sequence:**
    1.  **Initial Text:** Send a text stating, "Hi [Agent Name], this is [Your Name] with [Your Company]. How are you doing?"
    2.  **Follow-up Text (if no reply within a few hours):** "Hey, could you help me out or probably not?" or "Would you be available to give me a call today?" The goal is always to prompt a phone call.
    3.  **Sequence Length:** Send up to 5 texts in the initial sequence.
    4.  **60-Day Break:** If no response after 5 texts, the system waits 60 days.
    5.  **Second Sequence:** After 60 days, send another 5 texts, starting with a message like, "Are you still active with your license or not?" This often prompts a reply.
    6.  **Automation:** This sequence is automated using a system like GoHighLevel.
    7.  **Conversation Trigger:** Once an agent responds and you talk to them, they are removed from the automated funnel to begin a direct conversation.

-   **Agent Call Script:**
    1.  **Opening:** "Hey, [Agent Name], this is Hu with HBI Realty. How are you doing?" (Note: Hu uses "HBI Realty" because he has a license; if you don't, just use your name).
    2.  **Purpose:** "Awesome. I'm contacting you because me and my partner are looking to buy another investment property in the [market] area. Is this something you can help with?"
    3.  **Agent Response:** They will likely say, "Yes, it depends. What are you looking for? How can I help?"
    4.  **Your Needs:** "We're looking for properties with motivated sellers that want to sell fast for cash, or houses that need some work. The reason because my partner has a construction team to help me fix up the houses after we buy them, but it's been difficult lately to find any properties on the MLS (Multiple Listing Service) that makes sense.     5.  **If Agent Says No:** "I'm going to send you my contact information after we hang up so you can have it saved in your phone. If you have any properties coming up the pipeline in the future, please let me know. I would love to have you write the offers for us, and this way you can keep both sides of the commissions on all of your listings."
    6.  **Closing:** "I appreciate your time. Nice talking to you. Have a great day."

## Summary
This video provides valuable insights into effective strategies for real estate investors to connect with and acquire deals from real estate agents. The core advice revolves around targeting agents in areas adjacent to major metropolitan markets, rather than directly within them. This is because agents in these peripheral areas are typically less overwhelmed with calls from investors, making them more receptive to building relationships. While cash buyers from major cities are often willing to travel 45 minutes to an hour for good investment opportunities, these outlying agents are less frequently contacted, offering a less competitive environment for investors.

The speakers also delve into a systematic approach for agent follow-up, emphasizing the importance of moving beyond generic "checking in" messages. Instead, they advocate for sending engaging, open-ended questions about market conditions or economic trends to foster genuine conversations and build rapport. This consistent, yet varied, communication strategy aims to keep the investor top-of-mind, encouraging agents to proactively bring potential deals. The video underscores that success is not about a single perfect action but rather the cumulative effect of many small, consistent efforts in communication and relationship building, which can be further optimized through outsourcing and adapting to local market nuances.

## Key Takeaways
-   **Target Adjacent Markets:** Focus on real estate agents in areas 45 minutes to an hour outside major metropolitan markets, as they are less saturated with investor calls and more receptive.
-   **Cash Buyers' Flexibility:** Cash buyers are often willing to travel up to an hour outside major metros for good investment deals, making properties in adjacent areas attractive.
-   **Engaging Follow-up Texts:** Replace generic "checking in" texts with open-ended questions about the market or economy to foster genuine conversations and build rapport with agents.
-   **Consistency is Key:** Success comes from consistently implementing many small, strategic efforts in communication, which compound over time to yield significant results.
-   **Outsource Cold Calling:** Consider hiring a cold caller to manage follow-ups, as agents often don't remember individual voices, freeing up acquisition managers for more critical tasks.
-   **Leverage Local Accents:** In certain markets (e.g., the South), hiring cold callers with a local accent can significantly improve comfort levels and response rates.
-   **Differentiate Your Approach:** Clearly communicate to agents that you are a serious buyer focused on motivated sellers or properties needing cash offers, avoiding time-wasting activities like driving around to look at unsuitable properties.
-   **Commission Structure for Off-Market Deals:** For off-market properties, offer to pay the buyer's side commission (e.g., 3%) or suggest the seller reduce the price by that amount. Alternatively, consider offering the agent the full 6% commission as if they were double-dipping.
-   **Build a Large Pipeline:** Aim to have at least 500 agents in your system to start seeing consistent deal flow (e.g., one deal per month or every two months).

## Frameworks / SOPs
-   **Agent Outreach Strategy (Targeting Adjacent Markets):**
    1.  **Identify Target Areas:** Pinpoint counties or regions located approximately 45 minutes to an hour's drive from major metropolitan centers.
    2.  **Agent Identification:** Find real estate agents operating within these identified adjacent areas.

## Key Takeaways
-   **Higher Agent Commissions**: Offering 6% or even 10% commission to agents can greatly incentivize them to bring off-market deals and aggressively pitch your offer to sellers.
-   **Seller Perception**: Position the offer such that the seller pays no commission, as the investor covers it. This is a strong value add for the seller, even if the overall purchase price is adjusted.
-   **Structured Lead-to-Deal Process**: Implement a multi-phase system (cold calling, discovery call, closing) to efficiently manage and convert leads.
-   **Prioritize Seller Motivation**: During discovery calls, focus on uncovering the seller's motivation (distress, urgency, property condition) rather than just their asking price. High motivation can lead to a deal even with a high initial price.
-   **Property-Specific Buyer Broker Agreement (BBA)**: If an agent is hesitant to share a property address without a listing agreement, offer to sign a property-specific BBA to reassure them they will get paid.
-   **Justify Low Offers**: Use After Repair Value (ARV), repair costs, and property condition as talking points to justify your offer to the agent, ensuring they understand the rationale and advocate for you.
-   **Off-Market Advantage**: Deals sourced off-market through agents tend to have a better closing ratio (e.g., 1 in 15 offers) compared to on-market deals.
-   **Part-Time Feasibility**: The system can be built and run part-time, especially by leveraging agents for on-site tasks and using automated text models for initial outreach.

## Frameworks / SOPs
-   **Three-Phase Acquisition Process**: A structured approach for cold callers to evolve into acquisition managers and close deals.
    1.  **Cold Caller**: Initial outreach to agents to identify potential leads.
    2.  **Discovery Call**: A detailed call to gather information about the property and, crucially, the seller's motivation. This phase involves:
        *   **Build Rapport**: 15-30 seconds of friendly conversation.
        *   **Ask for Property Address**: If not provided, ask for a property-specific Buyer Broker Agreement (BBA) to reassure the agent about commission.

        *   **Assume Questions (if agent doesn't tell)**: Inquire about owner-occupancy, vacancy, condition, and necessary repairs.
        *   **Back Up to Ask Seller Motivation**: If motivation is still unclear, ask about the history or features that led the seller to decide to sell.
    3.  **Closing**: Making the offer, asking for a counter, and running numbers. The goal is to get the agent to agree with the investor's valuation based on ARV and rehab costs, so the agent will pitch the offer effectively to the seller.
