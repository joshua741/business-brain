# This EXACT Wholesale Real Estate Script Made $1.2M In 12 Months

## Summary
This video presents a detailed, step-by-step breakdown of a real estate wholesaling "Discovery Call Script" designed to secure distressed properties listed on the Multiple Listing Service (MLS). The speaker, Alex Martinez, CEO and Founder of RealEstateSkills.com, claims this exact script generated over $1.2 million in 12 months. The core strategy revolves around building rapport and incentivizing real estate agents by offering them the opportunity for dual commission or referral fees, thereby aligning interests and making the agent an ally in the transaction.

The script is structured as a "Two-Call Closing Framework," starting with a "Discovery Call" to gather crucial, non-public information about the property and seller's motivation, and then a "Close Call" to present a well-researched, calculated offer. Key aspects covered include warm introductions, understanding seller motivation (e.g., divorce, financial distress), assessing property condition (major issues, upgrades, special features), gauging the agent's estimated After Repair Value (ARV), and understanding the current market activity and competition. The video emphasizes the importance of asking precise questions to gain leverage and make the most accommodating offer possible, rather than just the highest.

## Key Takeaways
-   **Incentivize Real Estate Agents:** Agents are motivated to close deals because they only get paid upon closing. Offering them dual commission (if you're not an agent and they represent you as a buyer's agent) or a referral fee (if they can't represent you but refer another agent) significantly increases their incentive to favor your offer.
-   **Early Representation Discussion:** Always ask the agent if they can represent you as a buyer's agent early in the discovery call. Waiting until the end can lead to missed opportunities and frustration from the agent.
-   **Gather Seller Motivation:** Ask "What's the seller's reason for selling?" to uncover non-public information (e.g., divorce, financial distress, urgency) that provides leverage for your offer.
-   **Thorough Property Condition Inquiry:** Ask detailed questions about the property's overall condition, major structural/foundational/electrical issues, and recent upgrades. This helps accurately calculate repair budgets and identify leverage points.
-   **Understand Agent's ARV:** Ask the agent what they believe the home could be worth once renovated to gauge their understanding of the investment potential and their education level in real estate investing.
-   **Assess Market Activity & Competition:** Inquire about the current activity (calls, offers), the type of offers (homeowner vs. investor), and the number of offers to understand your competition and urgency.
-   **Determine Purchase Price Flexibility:** Directly ask "What price do I have to come in at today to get it under contract?" and follow up with "How flexible is that price?" and "Is the seller willing to review offers less than that price?" to find the lowest acceptable price.
-   **Be Flexible with Offer Terms:** Ask about specific offer terms the seller would like (e.g., longer closing period, shorter inspection contingency) to make your offer more accommodating.
-   **Know Seller's Review Plan:** Always ask "When does the seller plan on reviewing and deciding on offers?" to create urgency and ensure you can adjust your offer or follow up promptly.
-   **Extract Off-Market Deals:** Conclude the discovery call by asking if the agent knows of any other fixer-upper properties (on or off-market) they could represent you on, to generate additional deal flow.
-   **Don't Make an Offer on the Discovery Call:** The first call is for information gathering. Make a calculated, justified offer on a second "Close Call" after thorough analysis.
-   **Handle Objections as Deal Multipliers:** View objections (e.g., "offer is too low," "seller wants highest and best") as opportunities to educate the agent, reframe your offer, and demonstrate professionalism, which can multiply your accepted offers.
-   **Leverage Statistics:** Use statistics like "75% of all houses sell below the list price" to educate agents who may have misconceptions about market dynamics.
-   **Be Persistent and Professional:** Follow up diligently and present yourself as a serious, knowledgeable buyer who builds long-term relationships.

## Frameworks / SOPs
-   **Discovery Call Script:** A structured script used for the initial phone call with a real estate agent on a distressed property. Its purpose is to gather comprehensive information about the property, the seller's motivation, market activity, and the agent's expectations, while building rapport and incentivizing the agent. This call is not for making an offer, but for collecting data to formulate a strategic offer.
-   **Two-Call Closing Framework:** This is the overarching process. The first call is the "Discovery Call" (as described above). The second call, the "Close Call," is made after analyzing all the gathered information to present a well-researched, non-negotiable offer. This framework ensures that offers are calculated, justifiable, and presented professionally, increasing the likelihood of acceptance.
-   **B.A.M.F.A.M. (Book A Meeting From A Meeting):** A pro tip within the Discovery Call. If the agent is busy and cannot talk immediately, instead of ending the call, schedule a specific time for a follow-up meeting. This ensures continued engagement and capitalizes on the agent's incentive to close the deal.
-   **Deal Extraction Method (Agent Outreach):** A strategy to generate off-market or coming-soon deals. At the end of the Discovery Call, ask the agent if they know of any other fixer-upper properties (on or off-market) that they could represent you on. This leverages the established rapport and incentivized relationship to expand your deal pipeline beyond just active MLS listings.
