## 1. Connection Questions

*The NEPQ Connection Questions take the focus off of you and puts it on the seller and helps to lower their guard.*

### Cold Calling

> "Hey Bob? Yeah it's just Jon calling from Orange County…. I'm holding a copy of your property tax records on your home there on 123 Main St, and I was wondering if you could… possibly… help me out for a moment?"

> "Yeah and I'm not even sure if it makes sense for us to talk, I represent a group of buyers who are buying 5 - 6 different homes in that 4 block area that your Main St home is in… and after seeing yours they had me call you to see if you'd be… opposed to having a brief conversation about the Main St home… I guess would you be opposed to that?"

> Status Frame: "Yeah and this first call is pretty basic, it's really more for us to find out a little bit more about the property, you know like the condition it's in and really when you would be able to sell the property if you did.. And at the end of this call if you feel like it might make sense to go over an offer, then we could talk about possible next steps, would that help?"

### Outbound Leads (Appointment set by cold caller or SMS blast)

> "Hey Bob? Yeah it's just Jon, Jon Lallande calling from ABC Home Buyers, it looks like you just spoke to Jasmin (Cold Caller) about possibly selling your home on Main St so you can move down to Florida closer to your kids?"

> "And I don't want to assume anything here but what was the main thing that you went over with Jasmin, that caused you to… maybe… want to look into this a bit further?"

### Inbound Leads (Form online or pay per lead source)

> "Hey Bob? Yeah it's just Jon, Jon Lallande calling from ABC Home Buyers, it looks like you just filled out a form on our website about possibly selling your home on Main St, is that right?"

> "And it looks like you found us on Youtube? Ah, and what was it about the ad or what you saw online that caused you to… maybe… want to look into this… a bit further?"

### Answering Inbound Calls (Direct mail/TV/Google/radio ad)

> "This is Jon with ABC Homebuyers, how may I help you?"
> "Ok and did you have a property you were looking to sell? What's the address of that property? And your name is?"

## 2. Situation Questions

*Helps understand their current situation and helps sellers discover problems they might be unaware of.*

**First Situation Question**: "So the house… Do you live there… Is it a rental for you... What's going on with it?"

### Homeowners
- "Where are you headed next?"
- "Why there, though?"
- "How long have you been wanting to move out there to be closer to your grandkids?"
- If no destination: "What's the main reason you might want to move on from this one?"

### Investors
- "The tenants, are they month to month, on a lease, what's going on?"
- "And how much are they paying?"
- "Are they paying every month, on time, you know like the exact time they're supposed to?"
- "How are the tenants?"

### Vacant Properties
- "How long has it been vacant though?"
- "What are you doing now to keep up with the maintenance… you know like the yard, exterior, things like that?"
- "Are you the one covering all of those expenses or who's actually responsible for paying all of that?"
- "How long have you been covering all of those expenses?"

## 3. Problem Awareness Questions

*Helps understand what the sellers' real problems are, the root cause, and how these problems are negatively affecting them.*

### Unclear Motivation (common in cold calling/outbound)

- "So do you… like… the house you have now?"
- If yes: "What do you like about it?" then "Is there anything you'd change about the home, the location it's in, or the return you're getting if you could?"
- If no: "What don't you like about it?"

### Motivation is Clear

- "So you've been dealing with _________ for quite some time now… what came up recently that caused you to want to… possibly… make a change… now?"
- "What's the main reason you're looking to work with an investor like ourselves instead of… I don't know… taking a gamble… throwing it on the market… and hoping for the best with that route?"
- "Do you want to keep dealing with _________ if you… didn't have to?" → "Why not though?"

## 4. Clarifying and Probing Questions

*Sellers tend to give vague generalized answers. Probing questions allow us to go deeper.*

- Mirror: Seller says "It's just been too much" → respond with "Too much?..."
- "How do you mean?"
- "How long has that been going on though?"
- "What's that doing to you?"
- "I know if I were dealing with that, it would be… Really… Stressful… What's that doing to you though?"
- "So it sounds like it's possibly time to… make a change?"

## 5. Solution Awareness Questions

*Two purposes: (1) prevent objections before they come up, (2) help seller focus on their future after problems are solved.*

### First Half

- "So ideally when would you like to move on from this house so you no longer have to deal with _________ ?"
- "And before speaking to me, what have you tried to do so far so that you could move on from this property?"
- If nothing tried: "What's prevented you from taking care of this in the past though?"
- If talking to agents: "That's a fairly decent option… What's the main reason you haven't moved forward with them?"
- If more money answer: "And let's just say all of the companies come back with the same price… how would you then decide on who to go with?"

### Second Half

- "Ok so let's say that you're able to sell the home and you're no longer dealing with _________ .... What's your main goal with the new home/the next investment/money you get from the sale?"
- "Ok so let's say that you're able to sell the home and you're no longer dealing with _________ and you're finally able to _________ ... what are you looking forward to… the most… about that next chapter of your life?"
- "How do you see things being different with the new house than they are right now with this current one?"
- "How's that going to feel once you're _________ ?"

## 6. Needs Questions

*When sellers are focused on NEEDS instead of WANTS: more likely to make decisions, helps get steeper discounts.*

- "So what do you feel like you need in your pocket after all is said and done so that you can _________ ?"
- "So what is it that you need in your pocket so you no longer have to deal with _________ ?"

---

## 7. Anchor Questions

*Sellers often tell you they need more money than they actually do. The anchor gets the real number and prevents sticker shock.*

- "Well I'm going to do everything I can to get every penny of that _______ that you said that you needed… Now let's say for whatever reason all of the offers come back with a number lower than that… like… I don't know…. $XXX,XXXX (anchor at 80% of your offer or 80% of their needs)... should I just tell them to kick rocks and not even call you back or would you still be able to _________ ?"

---

## 8. Consequence Questions

*Gets sellers to defend themselves on why they need to change NOW. Creates urgency and empowers them to decide.*

- "Have you considered what would happen if you don't do anything about this?"
- "What would happen if we keep pushing this off like we have been and you continue to _________ ?"
- "_________ is definitely on the table… have you considered what would happen if you do _________ and _________ ends up getting worse?"

---

## 9. Commitment Questions

*Used after consequence questions to get the seller to commit to making a change.*

- "How important is it for you to be able to _________ ?"
- "So it sounds like it's important for you to do something about this then?"
- "So it sounds like it's time to make a change?"
- "Are you committed to doing something about this so that you don't have to keep dealing with _________ ?"

---

## 10. Deal Killers

*After preventing big objections, cover all logistical concerns so they don't become objections later.*

- "How soon are you wanting to sell this property so that way you can _________ ?"
- "And are you going to be able to get all of your stuff out in that time?"
- "How long would it take you to move all of your stuff to the new house?"
- "Who else would want to know that you're selling this house?"
- If tenants: "How do you feel your tenants are going to react when you tell them you're going to sell the home? What happens if they start causing trouble?"

---

## 11. The Stress Test

*Closes the deal before presenting the offer. Ensures every objection is overcome.*

- "So let's say we can get you _________ so that you no longer have to deal with _________ ... and we can do that in the next ___ days all while taking care of _________ ... What would be your biggest concern at that point?"
- If "Nothing": "What would you want the next steps to be then?"

---

## 12. Transition to Close

- "Here's what I'll do from here, I'll send this to my team and outline everything that we spoke about… and if it looks like something we can help with… I'll come back and walk you through the next steps… would that be helpful?"
- "Here's what I'll do from here, I'll go review some of the comparables in the area and run through the numbers… and if it looks like something we can help with… I'll type up an agreement outlining everything that we discussed. Would that help?"

---

## 13. Presentation Phase

*Present the offer from a place of helping them — not throwing a number and hoping it sticks. Start at 90-95% of actual offer.*

- "Ok so it looks like we are able to help you with this… and after reviewing this with our investors it looks like the best offer you could get so that way you could _________ is $_________ . Now that's what you'll walk away with in your pocket minus your mortgage or any property taxes you owe all while helping do this in the next ___ days and _________ (Solving their concerns)... Do you feel like that could be the… answer for you?"
