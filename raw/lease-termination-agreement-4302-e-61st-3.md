LEASE TERMINATION AGREEMENT
4302 East 61st Street, Lubbock, Texas
PARTIES Landlord: W&M Series LLC - E 61st Street Series (the Landlord).
Tenant: Jalissa Ramos Mendoza (the Tenant).
Property: 4302 East 61st Street, Lubbock, Texas.
AGREEMENT This Lease Termination Agreement is entered into between the parties identified above with respect to the Tenant's current lease at the Property. Both parties enter this Agreement in good faith and with the mutual intent to accommodate Tenant's request to terminate her lease while preserving Tenant's ability to return to the Property under her existing lease terms where possible. The parties agree to the following terms governing the termination of that lease and any options for reinstatement.
1. Termination Fee and Effective Date of Agreement In connection with the termination of Tenant's lease, a termination fee of $3,000.00 is due and payable to Landlord. This Agreement shall only become effective and binding upon Landlord's confirmed receipt of the full $3,000.00 termination fee. Until such payment is received, this Agreement is not enforceable by either party.
2. Consequences for Failure to Pay Termination Fee Landlord enters this Agreement in good faith and does not wish to pursue adverse action against Tenant. However, if the $3,000.00 termination fee is not received and Tenant vacates or abandons the Property without completing this Agreement, Landlord reserves the right to pursue the following remedies:
a) Reporting of outstanding balance to credit agencies: The unpaid termination fee and any remaining lease obligations may be reported to one or more credit reporting agencies, which may negatively affect Tenant's credit profile.
b) Eviction filing: Landlord may initiate formal eviction proceedings, which may result in an eviction record appearing on Tenant's rental history and background reports.
c) Disclosure to property management networks: Tenant's information and account status may be shared with other property management companies and landlords as permitted by applicable law.
Landlord's preference is to resolve this matter cooperatively. The remedies above are outlined solely to ensure both parties understand the consequences of non-compliance, and Landlord will make every reasonable effort to avoid pursuing them.
3. Option to Reinstate — Written Notice Required Before June 30 Tenant has the option to reinstate her lease by providing written notice to Landlord before June 30. Because Tenant's monthly rent payment is due on the 25th of each month, the following applies depending on when written notice is received:

a) Notice received before June 25: Landlord will reinstate Tenant's lease. The full $3,000.00 termination fee will be credited toward Tenant's future lease payments or applied toward the balance of any seller finance agreement between the parties, at Tenant's election. b) Notice received on or after June 25 but before June 30: Tenant's June 25 payment will first be deducted from the $3,000.00 termination fee held by Landlord. The remaining credit balance will then be applied toward Tenant's future lease payments or toward the balance of any seller finance agreement, at Tenant's election. Landlord will reinstate Tenant's lease upon receipt of such notice. c) Written notice of reinstatement must be received in writing. Verbal notice will not be accepted.
4. Reinstatement Window After June 30 — Extended Through July 25 If Tenant does not provide written notice of reinstatement before June 30, Landlord reserves the right to begin actively marketing the Property to prospective tenants or buyers. Notwithstanding such marketing activity, Tenant retains a limited window to reinstate her lease through July 25, subject to the following conditions: a) Second deposit received before July 25: If Landlord receives a deposit from a new prospective tenant or buyer before July 25, Tenant's option to reinstate is immediately and permanently waived upon receipt of that deposit, regardless of the July 25 date. b) No reinstatement by July 25: If Tenant has not provided written notice of reinstatement by July 25, this termination agreement becomes permanent and irrevocable as of that date, regardless of whether the Property has been re-leased or re-sold. Tenant's reinstatement option expires upon whichever occurs first: (i) Landlord's receipt of a second deposit from a new tenant or buyer, or (ii) July 25.
5. Credit Applied After June 25 Payment If Tenant provides written notice of reinstatement after the June 25 payment has been processed, that payment amount will be deducted from the $3,000.00 termination fee. Any remaining credit balance may, at Tenant's election, be applied toward: a) Any future scheduled payment; b) Any past-due balance owed under the lease; or c) Tenant's seller finance agreement terms.
6. Entire Agreement This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties with respect to the termination of the lease at the Property and supersedes all prior discussions or representations. Any modification must be made in writing and signed by both parties.
