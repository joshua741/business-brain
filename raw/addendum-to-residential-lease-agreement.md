## AS-IS CONDITION AND TENANT REPAIR RESPONSIBILITIES

**Section 1.1 — Acceptance of Property in AS-IS Condition**
Tenant accepts the property in its AS-IS condition, WITH ALL FAULTS, as of commencement of the Lease term. No warranty or representation by Landlord as to condition, fitness, or code compliance — except as required by Texas law (Texas Property Code § 92.052).

**Section 1.2 — Tenant's Exclusive Responsibility for Repairs**
Per Texas Property Code § 92.006, tenant agrees to repair, maintain, and replace ALL portions of the property at tenant's sole cost — including structural components, systems, appliances, fixtures, landscaping. Landlord has no duty to perform repairs.

- Tenant must notify Landlord in writing of needed repairs
- Tenant shall NOT withhold rent or use self-help remedies without Landlord's prior written consent
- Failure to repair within 30 days of notice = material breach = grounds for eviction

**Section 1.3 — Compliance with Law**
All repairs must comply with applicable laws and obtain necessary permits. Tenant indemnifies Landlord from any non-compliance claims.

---

## INSURANCE PROVISIONS

**Section 2.1 — Landlord's Insurance**
Landlord maintains a Landlord Insurance Policy covering certain major repairs (fire, windstorm, hail). Landlord's sole obligation is to maintain the policy — no other insurance obligations.

**Section 2.2 — Tenant's Responsibility for Deductible**
If Landlord files a claim (at Landlord's sole discretion), Tenant is responsible for the full deductible amount. Must be paid within 5 days of demand.

**Section 2.3 — Landlord's Discretion Regarding Claims**
Landlord has absolute right to deny or not pursue any insurance claim. Tenant cannot compel Landlord to file a claim.

**Section 2.6 — Tenant's Renters Insurance Requirement**
Tenant must maintain renters insurance: minimum $100,000 liability per occurrence, with Landlord named as additional insured. Must provide proof before occupancy and at each renewal.

---

## CONTRACTOR APPROVAL

**Section 3.1**
Landlord has exclusive right to approve or deny any contractor. All work must be by licensed, bonded, insured professionals where required. Unauthorized work may result in eviction and liability for damages.
