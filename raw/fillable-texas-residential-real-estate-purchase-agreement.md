# Fillable Texas Residential Real Estate Purchase Agreement

**Summary**: Standard 8-page fillable Texas residential purchase agreement template covering buyer/seller terms, financing options (cash, bank, seller finance), earnest money, closing, title, property condition, and dispute resolution.

## Agreement Structure

### 1. Buyer and Seller
Identifies Buyer and Seller parties, mailing addresses, and effective date.

### 2. The Property
Property street address, type (Single-Family, Condo, PUD, Duplex, Triplex, Fourplex, Other), tax parcel ID, personal property included, and fixtures excluded.

### 3. Purchase Price
Three financing options:
- **All Cash Offer** — Buyer provides written third-party proof of funds within specified date; Seller has 3 business days to object.
- **Bank Financing** — Conventional, FHA, VA, or Other loan; Buyer must provide lender approval letter by specified date; contingent on Buyer qualifying (rate availability not a contingency).
- **Seller Financing** — Specifies loan amount, down payment, interest rate (% per annum), term (months/years), and required documentation timeline.

### 4. Earnest Money
Amount due by specified date; applied to Purchase Price at Closing; optionally held in separate escrow per Texas law.

### 5. Sale of Another Property
Option to make contingent on sale of another property within X days.

### 6. Closing Costs
Allocated to Buyer, Seller, or Both. Covers title search, deed preparation, transfer taxes, recording fees.

### 7. Funds at Closing
Must be cash, electronic transfer, money order, certified/cashier's check from in-state institution.

### 8. Closing
Closing date, time, and title company to be agreed upon. Taxes, rents, dues prorated at Closing.

### 9. Survey
Buyer may obtain survey before Closing; Seller must cure defects or Buyer may cancel with Earnest Money returned.

### 10. Mineral Rights
All subsurface rights (water, gas, oil, minerals) transfer to Buyer at Closing.

### 11. Title
Warranty deed conveyance; Seller orders Title Search Report; Buyer has X days to object; Buyer receives owner's title insurance at Closing.

### 12. Property Condition
Seller maintains property until Closing; Buyer performs own inspections by specified deadline; AS IS after Closing.

### 13. Seller's Indemnification
After recording, Buyer accepts AS IS WHERE IS; Seller makes no warranty beyond Agreement and disclosure forms.

### 14. Appraisal
Option: contingent or not contingent on appraisal meeting Purchase Price. Negotiation Period if appraisal is short.

### 15. Required Documents
Parties authorize all closing documents in good faith.

### 16. Termination
Absent default, Earnest Money released to Seller within X business days on termination.

### 17. Sex Offenders
Federal disclosure per SORNA registration requirements.

### 18. Time
Time is of the essence. Agreement is entire expression of parties' intent.

### 19. Buyer's Default
Liquidated damages = Earnest Money; sole remedy for Seller.

### 20. Seller's Default
Buyer may cancel and recover damages, or elect specific performance.

### 21. Earnest Money Dispute
Disputes submitted to mediation per Section 22.

### 22. Dispute Resolution
Three-step: Mediation → Arbitration (retired judge or 5-yr RE attorney) → Court. Exclusions: foreclosure, eviction, mechanic's lien, probate/small claims/bankruptcy.

### 23. Governing Law
State of Texas.

### 24–32. Additional Terms
Standard boilerplate: offer terms, binding effect, severability, offer expiration, acceptance, agent compensation, disclosures (Lead-Based Paint option), entire agreement clause.

### 33. Signature
Seller (x2), Buyer (x2), Agent (x2) — date and print name lines.
