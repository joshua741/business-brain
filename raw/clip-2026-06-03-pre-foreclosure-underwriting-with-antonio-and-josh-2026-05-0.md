## Summary
This video provides a detailed explanation of how to calculate cash flow for properties, specifically focusing on rent-to-own and seller-financed deals. The speaker outlines a series of "rules of thumb" for determining appropriate selling prices and corresponding interest rates based on the After Repair Value (ARV) of a property. He emphasizes that these calculations are not credit-based but rather interest-rate driven, with the goal of gradually increasing interest rates as the business acquires more properties.

The core of the lesson delves into the components of a mortgage payment, known as PITI (Principal, Interest, Taxes, Insurance), and the occasional inclusion of Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI). The speaker demonstrates how to use a loan calculator to derive the principal and interest, and provides guidelines for estimating taxes, insurance, and PMI. A key takeaway is the importance of calculating a "net margin" by subtracting the underlying mortgage payment and property management fees from the selling rent amount, aiming for a 20% profit margin to ensure the business's scalability and long-term success.

## Key Takeaways
-   Properties are sold for 105-110% of their After Repair Value (ARV).
-   Interest rates are determined by ARV: 9.5% for ARV 120k-150k, 9% for ARV 151k-210k, and 8.5% for ARV 211k+.
-   Mortgage payments consist of PITI (Principal, Interest, Taxes, Insurance) and sometimes Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI).
-   PMI is required if the down payment is less than 20% of the property's value.
-   For rent-to-own, calculate the borrower's PITI and round it up to the highest tenth to determine the selling rent amount.
-   Rent-to-own deposits are typically around 3.5% down.
-   Avoid "analysis paralysis" by not running detailed numbers on properties until after speaking with potential sellers and gathering initial information.
-   Property management fees are typically 2.5% of the selling rent amount.
-   The goal for the net margin on a property is 20% of the selling rent amount.
-   Maintaining a healthy net margin is crucial for scaling the business and ensuring profitability, allowing for reinvestment into the team and software.

## Frameworks / SOPs
-   **Cash Flow Calculation Method for Rent-to-Own / Seller Financing**:
    1.  **Determine Selling Price**: Properties are sold for 105-110% of their After Repair Value (ARV).
    2.  **Assign Interest Rate (Rule of Thumb)**:
        *   ARV 120k-150k: 9.5% interest
        *   ARV 151k-210k: 9% interest
        *   ARV 211k+: 8.5% interest
    3.  **Calculate Borrower's Escrow Payment (PITI)**: The borrower's escrow payment is designed to be the same as the underlying mortgage's PITI. PITI includes Principal, Interest, Taxes, Insurance, and sometimes Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI). PMI is typically required if less than 20% is put down.
    4.  **Calculate Rent-to-Own Payment**: Calculate the borrower's PITI and then round it to the highest tenth (e.g., $1697.68 becomes $1700). This rounded figure is used as the selling rent amount.
    5.  **Determine Rent-to-Own Deposit**: Deposits are typically 3.5% down.
    6.  **Estimate Insurance (Rule of Thumb)**:
        *   ARV 120k-150k: ~$200 per month
        *   ARV 151k-210k: ~$250-$300 per month
        *   ARV 211k-300k+: ~$250-$350 per month
    7.  **Estimate PMI (Rule of Thumb)**:
        *   ARV 120k-150k: ~$100 per month
        *   ARV 151k-210k: ~$150 per month
        *   ARV 211k-300k+: ~$175-$200 per month
    8.  **Calculate Actual Net Margin**:
        *   **Selling Rent Amount**: The rounded PITI from step 4.
        *   **Property Manager Fee**: 2.5% of the Selling Rent Amount.
        *   **Net Gross Rent**: Selling Rent Amount - Property Manager Fee.
        *   **True Net**: Net Gross Rent - (Actual Mortgage PITI).
        *   **Net Margin Goal**: Aim for 20% of the Selling Rent Amount.
