# Selling Notes / Partials

**Summary**: WIH's next strategic tier — instead of selling a property outright, sell the future payment stream (the note), including "partials" (selling a portion of the payments). Lives on the payment side rather than the asset value side.

**Sources**: transcripts 2026-06-24, 2026-06-26

**Last updated**: 2026-06-26

---

- Concept: rent → seller-finance to a buyer (collect a down payment) → then **sell partials** of the note to an investor. Because total payments (principal + interest over the term) far exceed the face value, selling the payments can net more than a straight wholesale of the property.
- Example run on **[[3423-e-baylor-st]]**: a straight investor cash sale nets ~$2–6k; structuring + selling partials was modeled to net materially more (low-five-figures to low-twenties on the example). This is why clean, systematized note/servicing records (first + second position + servicing) matter — see [[mortgage-status-sheet]].
- Ties to the company roadmap goal of originating and **servicing paper in-house**. See [[company-roadmap]], [[creative-financing]].
