# Infinite Banking

**Summary**: Whole-life insurance used as a tax-advantaged "private bank" — cash value grows and can be borrowed against to fund real estate deals. Joshua holds policies across Lafayette Life, OneAmerica, and SML (plus Guardian referenced).

**Sources**: 25k-dump-in-280-per-mo-lafayette-life-insurance-policy-6-14-2025.md; policy-resource-guide.md

**Last updated**: 2026-05-29

---

The Infinite Banking Concept (IBC) / "Bank On Yourself" strategy: max-funded whole-life policies with Paid-Up Additions (PUA) riders accumulate cash value that can be borrowed against for investment capital and repaid — an alternative capital source alongside real estate and [[pml-tl]] lending.

## Policies
- **Lafayette Life** — whole life with PUA rider, **$25,000 lump-sum "dump-in" + $280/mo**, dated June 14, 2025 (source: 25k-dump-in-280-per-mo-lafayette-life-insurance-policy-6-14-2025.md).
- **OneAmerica, SML** (and Guardian) — additional carriers with policy-management resources (loan requests, direct deposit, PUA funding) catalogued in the policy resource guide (source: policy-resource-guide.md).

## 4 rules of policy management
1. Pay premiums. 2. Max-fund the PUA rider. 3. Pay policy-loan interest yearly. 4. Pay back loans (source: policy-resource-guide.md).
