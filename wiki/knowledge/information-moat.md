# Information Moat

**Summary**: The strategic idea that durable AI advantage comes not from optimized prompts but from the quality and depth of the data, context, and systems you feed the model — reframing the [[business-brain]] as Joshua's single most important durable asset.

**Sources**: clip-2026-06-03-how-claude-code-s-creator-starts-every-project.md, clip-2026-06-03-the-new-way-of-making-content-in-the-age-of-ai.md

**Last updated**: 2026-06-03

---

From [[boris-cherny]] (via Rich Sutton's "The Bitter Lesson"): the more general model always beats the specific one — "never bet against the model." Therefore durable advantage comes NOT from optimized prompts but from the quality and depth of the data, context, and systems you feed the model. That is your **information moat**.

For Joshua this reframes the [[business-brain]] wiki as his single most important durable asset. It also ties to Alex Hormozi's "do epic shit and document it" — proof as a moat (see [[content-ai]]). The deeper and more proprietary the context, the less replicable the AI output.

This idea underpins the [[ai-operating-system]] and [[ai-automation-strategy]].
