# Cleaning Business Models

**Summary**: Strategic framework comparing commercial, residential, and hybrid cleaning business models. Commercial B2B is the superior long-term play; residential is the entry ramp.

**Sources**: Commercial vs. Residential vs. Hybrid Cleaning Businesses A Comprehensive Strategic Analysis.md (Manus AI, 2026-05-29)

**Last updated**: 2026-05-29

---

## The Three Models

**Commercial (B2B Janitorial)**: Cleans offices, medical facilities, schools, and industrial spaces. Large contracts, legally binding 12-36 month terms, nightly/overnight work. Revenue ceiling of 250K-1M+ annually. Exit value 3.0x-5.5x EBITDA — PE rollup target.

**Residential (B2C House Cleaning)**: Cleans private homes. Small per-visit fees (100-300), immediate cash payment, M-F daytime hours. Max scale 75K-200K annually with a team. High churn (20-30%/yr), exit value only 1.5x-2.5x SDE.

**Hybrid**: Attempts to serve both markets simultaneously. Fails operationally — requires two separate labor forces on different schedules, two marketing budgets, and 24/7 owner presence. Graded F on staffing, marketing difficulty, and work-life balance.

## Why Commercial Wins Long-Term

**Contractual wealth**: Commercial revenue is bound by multi-year B2B contracts — predictable, legally protected cash flow that institutional buyers and PE firms pay 3.0x-5.5x EBITDA for. Residential has no contracts and commands only 1.5x-2.5x SDE.

**Institutional scalability**: A 1M-revenue commercial business can run on 5-10 high-value contracts. The equivalent residential operation needs hundreds of homes, dozens of teams, and massive admin overhead.

**Market protection**: Commercial compliance requirements (2M+ GL insurance, janitorial bonding, OSHA certifications) weed out amateur competitors. Residential has zero barrier to entry.

## Strategic Play for LBK Cleaners

Start residential via [[bookingkoala]] — fast cash flow, low startup, immediate revenue. Use cash to fund commercial equipment (industrial vacuums, floor scrubbers, 2M+ GL insurance). Transition client mix toward commercial B2B over 12-24 months. Medical offices (OSHA-certified) command 25-50% premium margins over standard offices.

The report explicitly recommends [[profit-first]] for this transition: cap labor at 50-55% of contract revenue, allocate 10-15% strictly to profit.
