# How To Start Wholesale Real Estate In 2026 (15 hrs/wk) — Alex Martinez

**Summary**: Alex Martinez (RealEstateSkills.com) lays out an on-market / MLS wholesaling model that needs no marketing spend and runs in ~15 hrs/wk, with a reverse-engineered KPI math and five tactics for finding hot distressed leads.

**Sources**: clip-2026-06-03-start-wholesale-2026-FULL.md

**Last updated**: 2026-06-03

## Core method / framework

ON-MARKET / MLS wholesaling — no marketing spend, ~15 hrs/wk. The whole model is built on reverse-engineering inputs from a target output.

**The reverse-engineering math:**
- ~$10K average wholesale fee = 1 deal/mo
- 1 deal/mo = 15 written offers/mo
- 15 offers/mo = ~0.5 offers/day
- Scale is linear: want 2x income → make 2x offers.

> "If you want to make $10,000 a month... you need 15 written offers in a month."

**15-hour weekly schedule:** 1 hr morning + 1 hr lunch + 1 hr evening, x5 days.

**4-step process:**
1. Find distressed houses
2. Submit offers to get under contract
3. Sell the rights to a cash buyer / flipper
4. Collect the wholesale fee ($10K+)

**MLS = "Multiple LEADS Service."** ~90% of transactions happen on-market with an agent; only ~10% are off-market. The MLS gives photos, full description, and — critically — **CONFIDENTIAL REMARKS** (seller motivation that does NOT show on Zillow/Redfin).

> "Multiple LEADS Service."

**MLS access options:** assistant access (~$24/qtr, no license needed) / Redfin / Realtor / Zillow / get a license / an agent login / Privvy.

**Finding HOT leads — 5 tactics:**
1. **Day-0 listings** — last-24-hour listings; call FAST.
2. **Old listings** — 60+ days on market.
3. **Keyword search** — cash, fixer, needs TLC, hoarder, fire damage, pre-foreclosure, divorce, estate, probate, vacant, deferred maintenance, mold, cracked foundation, squatters, late payments, DIY.
4. **Price reductions.**
5. **2/1 → 3/2 conversions.**
- Plus the "Coming Soon" filter.

> "Speed is the name of the game."
> "The longer a property sits, the more motivated that seller."

**Hierarchy of Representation (how you get paid / structure the deal):**
1. Listing agent represents you = dual commission (~$12K vs ~$6K), happens ~80% of the time.
2. LA refers you out for a referral fee (~25%, ~$1,500).
3. Represent yourself and waive the buyer-agent commission to net the seller more.

## Numbers & benchmarks

- Alex Year 1: $1.2M profit / $12M revenue / 50+ deals.
- First deal: 8 hours of work, $22K fee.
- Student Michael: $187K across 13 deals.
- Student Kevin Perkins: $7K → $110K/mo.
- Median days on market (DOM): 66.

- MLS access cost: ~$0–75/qtr.
