# Company Roadmap — The Billion-Dollar Ladder

**Summary**: The single north-star document for where everything rolls up. The goal is a billion-dollar, AI-run real estate empire. Today's businesses exist to throw off cash flow that funds each higher tier of the ladder.

**Sources**: Joshua, session 2026-06-03

**Last updated**: 2026-06-03

---

This page exists because nothing else captured the big picture. The individual verticals each have their own pages, but this is the ladder they climb — the thing an owner checks to confirm every tactical decision still serves the empire. **Goal: build this into a billion-dollar company.**

## The ladder (each tier funds the next)

**Tier 0 — Cash-flow engines (NOW).** Three businesses run in parallel, all in service of generating cash flow:
- [[lbk-cleaners]] — cleaning company, fastest path to near-term net income (camp fund → house fund).
- [[wholesale]] — top-of-funnel deal sourcing, AI-automated outreach ([[vince-ai]], [[ai-automation-strategy]]).
- [[rent-to-own]] / [[creative-financing]] — the current rental + seller-finance portfolio under [[wih]].

The point of Tier 0 is not these businesses for their own sake — it's the **serious cash flow** they produce to fund Tier 1.

**Tier 1 — New development.** Once cash flow is reliable, move into ground-up development. This is the first real wealth multiplier and the bridge to the capital tiers.

**Tier 2 — Capital deployment.** Development cash flow + reserves get redeployed into:
- **Private money lending** ([[pml-tl]]) — lend the cash out; capital earns on its own.
- **Commercial real estate** — bigger assets, bigger cash flow, the "empire" base.

**Tier 3 — Large-scale neighborhood development.** The top of the ladder: build entire neighborhoods, **vertically integrated** with in-house mortgage, [[rent-to-own]], and seller-finance products. WIH originates *and* services its own paper — operating on par with a mortgage company, not just a developer.

## Operating thesis (cuts across every tier)

**AI is the backbone — build an AI-run company.** Hire only (a) vital in-house roles, or (b) operators who are forceful multipliers *because* they use AI. Stay as resource-efficient on headcount as possible; AI does the leverage, people do what AI genuinely can't. See [[ai-operating-system]].

**Own the software — kill recurring/servicing/setup fees.** The long-term plan is to replace every paid SaaS with in-house apps. [[wih-app]] is the central CRM / one-stop shop. The next big build after the [[baselane-to-mercury-migration]] is an **in-house management app that replaces [[doorloop]] entirely** — tenant portals, in-house seller-finance note servicing (amortized schedules), and in-house rent-to-own payment collection/servicing. DoorLoop charges per user, and the business currently bleeds money on third-party servicing fees and setup costs; bringing it in-house is both a margin lever and a control lever. This is **much later** — only after the Mercury/banking integrations are fully in place — but it is a committed part of the plan.

**Financial backbone.** [[profit-first]] across all entities, banking consolidating onto [[mercury]] (see [[baselane-to-mercury-migration]]) so AI can own the money layer via API.
