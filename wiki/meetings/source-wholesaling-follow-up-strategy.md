# Wholesaling Houses: My Follow Up Strategy — Eric ($60M+ volume)

**Summary**: Eric (claims $60M+ wholesale volume) details a triple-dial-then-taper follow-up cadence, two qualification questions that gate the pipeline, and an objection rule for handling every objection before hanging up.

**Sources**: clip-2026-06-03-wholesaling-houses-my-follow-up-strategy.md

**Last updated**: 2026-06-03

---

## Core method / framework

**Triple-dial cadence (taper schedule):**
- First **2 weeks**: call EVERY day. Each attempt is a triple dial (call → no answer → call → no answer → call), then leave a voicemail AND a text every single time.
- Next **4 weeks**: once a week.
- After: once a month, indefinitely (he's closed leads after 5–6 years).

> "triple dial every single time... leave a voicemail and a text every single time."

**Two qualification questions before a lead enters the daily pipeline:**
1. "What type of deal could this be?" (wholesale, creative finance, renovation, listing program)
2. "When could this realistically happen?" (urgent vs long-term)

> "What type of deal could this be?... When could this realistically happen?"

**Objection rule:** Handle EVERY objection fully before hanging up —
> "Is it the price? Is it the terms? Is it me personally? Or is there another issue we haven't talked about?"

**CRM discipline:** Notes must instantly show what happened + what's next.

**Re-engagement:** Personalized selfie video + a "big claim" hook.

> "handle every objection fully before hanging up."

## Numbers & benchmarks

- $60M+ lifetime volume.
- Dozens of extra deals per year purely from follow-up.
- Cadence: 2 wk daily / 4 wk weekly / then monthly evergreen.
