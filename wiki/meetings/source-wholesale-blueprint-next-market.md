## Core method / framework

The No Fluff Model is 3 phases.

### Phase 1 — Lead Generation
Text and email agents at scale; an AI bot filters responses into buckets:
- **No answer** → automated follow-up
- **Hostile** → tag "Cash Agent Dead"
- **Conversation** → split into tiers:
  - **Tier 1** = gave an address (on- or off-market) → advance to Phase 2
  - **Tier 2** = gave an address but it didn't work out → automated follow-up
  - **Tier 3** = no deal/address but open to future business → different follow-up track

**"Gold"** = off-market + ugly house + asking price under Zillow's Zestimate.

### Phase 2 — Lead Conversion: the 3 Cs (Call, Comp, Close)
- **Call** — Lead Management Call ASAP, within 6 hours. "The sale happens on the first call." Build rapport, do NOT lead with an offer. Get the lay of the land: existing offers? agent's ARV opinion? scope of work? Respect the agent's opinion. Pre-frame: *"I'm probably going to be off on that number... are they flexible?"*
- **Comp** — underwrite within 12 hours; determine the max viable offer.
- **Close** — negotiate; only present to someone ready to decide. Give a verbal ballpark: *"I'm probably somewhere around 240, 245, 250... if it makes sense we'll put it in writing."*

### Phase 3 — Disposition
New investors JV. Key metric **TTB = Time To Blast** (contract → buyers). TTB < 12 hrs = >400% higher sell likelihood. Only 60–65% of contracts actually close. Expect a 30–45 day ramp; consistency compounds.

## Numbers & benchmarks
- 2026 market: +8.9% existing inventory
- Member Jonathan Smith: $126K/mo
- Average wholesaler spends $2K–10K/deal vs. No Fluff $100–150/deal
- 500 texts/day → 3–8 leads/day → 22–40% off-market → 12–20 leads per deal
- 3 deals/mo ≈ 60 off-market properties touched
- ~34–35 agents → 40–50 deals/yr
- TTB < 12 hrs = +400% sell likelihood
- 60–65% of contracts close
