## 5-Pillar Scale Strategy

**1. Leverage the Hub City Healthcare Ecosystem**
Lubbock anchors TTU, TTUHSC, Covenant Health, and UMC Health System. Do NOT bid on main hospital contracts first. Target the satellite ecosystem: independent PT clinics, dental practices, private medical outpatient buildings (MOBs). Winning 15-20 medical MOBs at 2,500/mo each = 500K+/yr in stable, high-margin revenue.

**2. Transition Owner to Systems-Manager by Month 6**
Stop cleaning. Move to sales, quality control, and hiring. Use [[profit-first]] from day one: cap labor at 50-55% of contract revenue, allocate 10-15% strictly to profit. Implement commercial cleaning software (Swept or Janitorial Manager) for GPS check-ins, digital checklists, and client inspection reports.

**3. Upsell Specialty Services (50-70% margins)**
Standard janitorial margin: 15-25%. Specialty floor care (strip/wax, carpet extraction) and window washing: 50-70%. Structure every contract to include standard janitorial plus periodic add-ons. Example: a dealership paying 3,500/mo nightly can be upsold 5,000/yr in scheduled floor buffing and pressure washing.

**4. Build CRE Referral Network**
Lubbock commercial brokers and property managers control most class-A and class-B office leases. Key targets: NAI Wheelhouse, CMS Properties, McDougal Companies. Offer rapid-response post-construction cleanups and move-out cleans. Good one-time service = preferred vendor referrals to new tenants for ongoing janitorial.

**5. Maintain Operational Buffer (The Restaurant Method)**
Keep 2-3 trained part-time cleaners on standby at premium hourly rate. When a new contract is signed, deploy standby team for launch. This protects existing clients, ensures flawless new client launch, and gives 30 days to hire and train permanent crew. Prevents the common failure of pulling best cleaners off existing accounts.
